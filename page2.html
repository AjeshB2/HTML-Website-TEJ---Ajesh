Building Process

Parts Used

  • Microbit
  • Servo motor
  • Cardboard box
  • Alligator clip to male jumper wires (3)
  • Power supply (I used a portable power bank)
  • Microbit USB cable
  • Duct tape

Hardware Connections

The Microbit is first connected to the servo motor via the alligator clips. These are connected so that the wires are clipped onto the Microbit's 0, 3V and GND pins. These are then connected to the motor using the male side of the wires. To power the device, the Microbit is plugged into a small power bank or any suitable power supply using its USB cable.

Coding

Input: Button A and button B both represent A and B in the letter combination. Both buttons are pressed together to confirm a combination.

Output: Pressing the right combination (ABBA) and confirming will open the door by turning the servo motor also displaying a checkmark on the microbit. Pressing a wrong combination and confirming will output an X on the microbit screen and play an alarm sound.
